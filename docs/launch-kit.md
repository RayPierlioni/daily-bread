## First 10 People To Ask

Start small. Ask people who will be honest and kind.

- One close friend
- One family member
- One pastor or ministry leader
- One small group leader
- One person who uses devotional apps
- One person who is newer to faith
- One person who has struggled with doubt
- One person who prays regularly
- One person who does not usually use apps like this
- One person who will notice confusing wording or buttons

## Gentle Launch Plan

### Day 1

Share the friend text message with 5 to 10 trusted people.

### Day 2

Ask them what felt helpful, confusing, or unnecessary.

### Day 3

Fix the most obvious problems.

### Day 4

Share the softer Facebook post.

### Day 5

Send the pastor or ministry leader message to one trusted church leader.

### Day 6

Create a simple QR code and one-page handout.

### Day 7

Review feedback and decide what feature or wording should improve next.

## What To Avoid

- Do not spam Christian groups.
- Do not post in groups that prohibit self-promotion.
- Do not describe the app as replacing church, pastors, counselors, or professional care.
- Do not make promises that the app will solve anxiety, grief, doubt, or pain.
- Do not pressure people to sign up.

## Best Positioning

Next Faithful Step should be introduced as a free spiritual rhythm tool, not as a social media platform or a replacement for church.

The safest message is:

Next Faithful Step helps people start the day with Scripture, prayer, honest questions, and private reflection.
